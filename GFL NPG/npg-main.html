INTRODUCTION

Girls' Frontline is a mobile game focused around asset development and management with strategic flair, whose primary strengths include the practicality of enjoying the game as a free-to-play participant. The game consistently rewards the progression of the dedicated, and is much more forgiving than the vast majority of gacha games on the market in terms of how much luck or money is required. That being said, one should aim to get the most possible from their time and effort, and this guide is here to help develop commanders capable of operating efficiently.

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES

Some of these may not mean much without the context, but it is a good idea to look them over beforehand to prevent possible mistakes.

GEMS: Gems are the premium currency of GFL. The absolute number one priority for these is buying two additional echelons, no exceptions.
ARSMG: Echelons formed from ARs and SMGs are the go-to choice for every new player-- almost every other composition will severely handicap a player's ability to progress quickly.
RARITY: Rarity is not necessarily representative of a doll's usefulness-- there exist both impractical high-rarity dolls and stellar-low rarity dolls.
PRODUCTION: One must take care to not produce dolls and equipment excessively, though new players should feel free to do so as much as needed provided that they reserve enough resources to continue grinding.
RESOURCES: In spite of what the currently displayed resource softcap is, one should always consider it to be 300,000-- the listed cap only applies to passive regeneration, not logistics (the commander's primary source of income). It is much more important to hoard resources for rate-up and grind events than to maintain passive regen by staying under the listed cap.

INITIAL PROGRESSION

The early game of GFL is characterized by raising one's first echelon (which should ideally be a versatile general-purpose ARSMG variant) and increasing resource income.

LOGISTICS

- Level arbitrary dolls for the purpose of sending out logistics missions early on. Always retain two echelons for active use (starting echelon count is four, so send two logis out to begin with).

Many missions will require the use of a 'dummy echelon', which is an echelon (usually one HG) used for the sole purpose of an extra movement point.

- Purchase fifth echelon when gem count permits. If not enough gems are available after acquiring the first three Anti-Rain dolls (see right), S rank some missions for extra gems.

There is no need to raise another echelon-- by the time story AR can be acquired, existing forces should be enough to handle relevant enemy strength, even when split up. Refer to below guide for more help and mission-specific references.

- Maintain logistics that provide doll contracts while continuing to craft with the recipe priority dictated on the right. Since the ones that give such contracts are initially inefficient resource-wise, players are advised to switch off them when not needed anymore.

This will vary from player to player to player, but a good rule of thumb is to stop non-daily crafting and doll contract rush whenever a sturdy main tank is produced (see right— "What should be crafted?" under "Pointers?") or when the first three AR Team dolls have been acquired, at which point one should just press on with grinding out levels (even suboptimal tanks will suffice).
ECHELON

- Aim to acquire the first three dolls of the Anti-Rain Team (henceforth "AR Team") by clearing 2-6, 3-3, and 3-6. Level a temporary ARSMG echelon formed with 2★ and 3★ dolls.

It is fortunate for new players that sufficiently strong friend supports can clear the required stages in the player's stead, so be sure to ask for help! Other notable though less stellar 2★ mentions are Gr G3 and L85A1 (ARs) as well as M38, Type 64, and Spectre M4 (SMGs). From these recommendations, the higher rarity dolls will perform better. Note that SMGs meant to serve as main tanks are denoted by (MT) tag; other SMGs serve as off-tanks.

- After acquiring the first three AR Team dolls, it is time to form a primary echelon committed to maximum linking/leveling. The baseline formation would be all three AR Team dolls shielded by two SMGs (see collapsible for more advice).

- If UMP (preferably 45, though 9 is still great) or Ingram are available, use as main tank. If not, use the Sten MkII provided as story reward.

- To avoid core hell, one 5★ doll at most should be used unless three of the dolls in formation are 3★ dolls or lower. It is generally not recommended to use any 5★ dolls if four of the dolls in formation are 4★.

- With this in mind, choose an offtank; Skorpion will suffice if no better choices are available (PPS-43, SR3-MP, Vector) or the team's core costs will be prohibitive.

- Similarly, choose who will serve as DPS ARs. Generally, the AR Team is recommended due to their value as the best 4★ ARs around (a great intersection of power and accessible cost), but if one insists on using a 5★ AR, the target for replacement would generally be best served by M4 SOPMODII.

- After getting the first ARSMG echelon to x5 links (or optionally max-leveled), one should proceed to either raise another ARSMG echelon, or an RFHG echelon. For the third echelon, raise whatever the type not chosen for the second.
- Note that with the raising of the first echelon finished, "corpse-dragging" becomes an option for those looking to save resources with extra effort.

This depends on one's priorities-- RFHG strongly augments one's night battle capabilities against armored units, however a second ARSMG can still fill this role relatively well while being more versatile across the board.

As a result, ARSMG is recommended particularly if gems are urgently needed (easier to get S ranks with) or a major event is around the corner (ARSMG is usually much more capable as an all-rounder).
