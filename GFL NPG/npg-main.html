GREETINGS, COMMANDER!

Welcome to Girl's Frontline! This guide will cover all the most important aspects of progressing through the game.

INTRODUCTION

Girls' Frontline is a mobile game focused around asset development and management with strategic flair, whose primary strengths include the practicality of enjoying the game as a free-to-play participant. The game consistently rewards the progression of the dedicated, and is much more forgiving than the vast majority of gacha games on the market in terms of how much luck or money is required. That being said, one should aim to get the most possible from their time and effort, and this guide is here to help develop commanders capable of operating efficiently.

Many thanks to those who have helped me along my own journey through GFL.

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES

Some of these may not mean much without the context, but it is a good idea to look them over beforehand to prevent possible mistakes.

GEMS:

Gems are the premium currency of GFL. The absolute number one priority for these is buying two additional echelons, no exceptions.

ARSMG:

Echelons formed from ARs and SMGs are the go-to choice for every new player-- almost every other composition will severely handicap a player's abaility to progress quickly.

RARITY:

Rarity is not necessarily representative of a doll's usefulness-- there exist both impractical high-rarity dolls and steller-low rarity dolls.

PRODUCTION:

One must take care to not produce dolls and equipment excessively, though new players should feel free to do so as much as needed provided that they reserve enough resources to continue grinding.

RESOURCES:

In spite of what the currently displayed resource softcap is, one should always consider it to be 300,000-- the listed cap only applies to passive regeneration, not logistics (the commander's primary source of income). It is much more important to hoard resources for rate-up and grind events than to maintain passive regen by staying under the listed cap.

INITIAL PROGRESSION

The early game of GFL is characterized by raising one's first echelon (which should ideally be a versatile general-purpose ARSMG variant) and increasing resource income.

LOGISTICS

- Level arbitrary dolls for the purpose of sending out logistics missions early on. Always retain two echelons for active use (starting echelon count is four, so send two logis out to begin with).

- Many missions will require the use of a 'dummy echelon', which is an echelon (usually one HG) for the sole purpose of an extra movement point.
